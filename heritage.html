Iconic Asian Heritage Sites

Explore the architectural wonders and historical landmarks that tell the stories of Asia's rich past.

India's Ancient Marvels

Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha is an impressive 13th-century temple dedicated to the sun god Surya. Designed as a colossal chariot with intricate carvings, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture, showcasing India's rich historical artistry.

Nalanda University

Nalanda University, located in Bihar, was a renowned Buddhist monastery and academic center from the 5th to 12th centuries CE. This UNESCO World Heritage Site was a global hub for learning, attracting scholars and students from across Asia to study philosophy, medicine, mathematics, and arts.

China's Historical Gems

Forbidden City

The imperial palace of China from the Ming dynasty to the end of the Qing dynasty. It served as the home of emperors and their households for almost 500 years.

Temple of Heaven

An imperial sacrificial altar in Beijing, this grand complex was where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties performed rituals to pay homage to Heaven and pray for good harvests.

Japan's Enduring Legacies

Himeji Castle

Japan's most magnificent surviving feudal castle, known for its pristine white exterior and complex, maze-like grounds. A quintessential example of Japanese castle architecture.

Historic Monuments of Ancient Kyoto

A collection of temples, shrines, and gardens, including the iconic Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), representing the cultural zenith of a millennium of imperial capital.
